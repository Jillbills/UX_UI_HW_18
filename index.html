Edúcate Ya Case Study


Edúcate Ya

Non-Profit Responsive Website Redesign

Edúcate Ya is a non-profit organization that helps educate Latinos to achieve better jobs and access to healthcare. We redesigned the non-profit organization’s website to be responsive and visually engaging to improve usability, navigation, and user engagement.










Project Overview

The Problem

Edúcate Ya hasn’t been reaching it’s targeted audience. Their homepage averaged an 85% bounce rate in Oct to Nov 2021, which is nearly twice as high as a typical website bounce rate.

The Solution

By redesigning Edúcate Ya’s website to be more visually engaging, we can help improve usability, navigation, and user engagement.




Our Team

Manny McIvor, UX/UI Design student,
Carlos Prieto, UX/UI Design student,
Jill Van, UX/UI Design student + Project Manager

We actively collaborated throughout the entire design process. As project manager, I facilitated the initial contact with the non-profit organization and setup stakeholder interviews. I contributed heavily to the UX Research phase by:


  • •Completing a Heuristic Evaluation of the current website

  • •Gathering interview insights, organizing into an Affinity Diagram

  • •Summarizing our Competitor Analyses

  • •Developing an Empathy Map

I also developed a UX Hypothesis, started the Information Architecture Sitemap, and built out a lot of the Mobile Mid-fi Wireframes and the styling and outline for our Case Study slides.

My teammates did a fabulous job creating a UX Scenario and portraying it through a well illustrated Storyboard. Manny took the lead on the Style Guide and menu Animation while Carlos ensured that our prototype was Responsive Design and he did the English - Spanish Translations. Together we utilized our skills and strengths to create a sucessful solution to our problem.
